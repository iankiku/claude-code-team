## Platform-Specific Shifts

### TikTok

**The Numbers**:
- 340% increase in B2B content engagement
- B2B buyers are humans who scroll TikTok
- Algorithm still friendly to new creators

**What's Working**:
```
Micro-moment demonstrations (7-15 seconds):
- Single product benefit
- Real-world scenario
- iPhone footage > production
- Direct-to-camera authenticity

Content types that convert:
- "Day in the life" featuring product
- Quick tips and workflow hacks
- Before/after transformations
- "POV: you just discovered [product]"

What's dead:
- Overproduced brand content
- Trend audio without relevance
- Dance challenges for B2B
- "Duet this" engagement bait
```

### LinkedIn

**The Shift**:
- Personal profiles outperform company pages 10x
- Long-form posts declining, visual content rising
- Founder-led content dominates B2B

**What's Working**:
```
Carousel posts:
- Story structure across slides
- Data visualizations
- Process breakdowns
- "Here's what I learned" frameworks

Video:
- Phone-filmed authenticity
- 60-90 second maximum
- Captions essential
- Direct teaching, not polished production

What's dead:
- Corporate announcements
- Motivational quotes
- "I'm humbled to announce..."
- Engagement pods
```

### YouTube Shorts

**The Opportunity**:
- Algorithm still rewarding new creators
- Less saturated than TikTok for B2B
- Cross-posts from TikTok perform well
- 60-second limit (vs. TikTok's 10 minutes)

### X (Twitter)

**Current State**:
- Engagement declining for brand accounts
- Personal accounts still effective
- Long-form posts (threads) declining
- Quick takes and hot takes perform best

## Community-First Growth

### The Reddit Strategy

**What Works**:
```
The 100:1 Rule:
- 100 genuinely helpful contributions
- 1 mention of your product (when relevant)
- Takes months before anyone cares

Subreddit approach:
1. Identify 5-10 relevant subreddits
2. Become known for helpful answers
3. Engage consistently for 3-6 months
4. Only mention product when genuinely solving problem
5. Never post promotional links

What gets you banned:
- Shilling too early
- Multiple accounts
- Obvious promotional posts
- Not disclosing affiliation
```

### Discord Communities

**What Works**:
```
Building the gathering place:
- Create space around topic, not product
- Genuine engagement from team members
- Regular events and programming
- Let community shape direction

Monetization path:
- Free community builds trust
- Premium tier for power users
- Product is natural upgrade, not pitch
```

## Content Authenticity

### The Death of Polish

```
2024: 4K camera, perfect lighting, scripted content
2026: iPhone footage, natural lighting, stream of consciousness

Why:
- Polish = advertisement (brain tunes out)
- Authenticity = trust signal
- Lo-fi = relatable
- Real > perfect
```

### Character and Lore

**The Duolingo Effect**:
- Brands building characters (Duo the owl)
- Internal lore and running jokes
- Consistency across platforms
- Fans participate in character development

**What This Means**:
```
Build a brand world:
- Recurring characters or personas
- Inside jokes for long-time followers
- Narrative arcs across content
- User participation in story

This beats:
- Jumping on every trend
- Generic corporate content
- Different voice across platforms
- Content without continuity
```

## Trust as Currency

### The New Reality

```
Trust > optimization

Users are:
- Ad-blind (decades of exposure)
- Skeptical of polish
- Sensitive to inauthenticity
- Looking for genuine connection

Winners are:
- Transparent about limitations
- Responsive to criticism
- Consistent in values
- Human in communication
```

### Building Trust Signals

| Trust Signal | How to Build |
|--------------|--------------|
| Founder presence | Regular, authentic content |
| Transparency | Public roadmaps, honest updates |
| Responsiveness | Fast, helpful support |
| Consistency | Same values across channels |
| User-generated proof | Reviews, case studies, testimonials |

## What's Dead in 2026

### Avoid These Patterns

```
Growth tactics that backfire:

❌ Aggressive pop-ups
   - Google penalizes
   - Users hate them
   - Conversion bump not worth brand damage

❌ Spray-and-pray email
   - Deliverability brutal in 2026
   - One spam complaint tanks domain reputation
   - Quality over quantity

❌ Trend-hopping without identity
   - Brands using every trending audio
   - No consistent voice
   - Forgettable and desperate-feeling

❌ "Viral challenges"
   - Unless naturally connected to product
   - Usually feels forced
   - Rarely converts to product usage

❌ Overproduced content
   - Signals "advertisement"
   - Brain automatically tunes out
   - Authenticity wins attention
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Authenticity > polish** - Lo-fi content from real people wins
2. **Community > campaigns** - Build gathering places, not ads
3. **Identity > trends** - Consistent character beats trend-chasing
4. **Trust > optimization** - Long-term relationship over short-term conversion
5. **Embedded > bolted-on** - Sharing should be natural to product use
